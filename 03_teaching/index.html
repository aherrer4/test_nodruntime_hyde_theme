Teaching

Network Infrastructures Labs

The aim of the course is to give students the ability to configure and debug network facilities on Unix-Like operating systems. We use Netkit, an open-source emulation framework for Linux boxes. The target audience are students at the 1-st year of M.Sc. in Cybersecurity, Computer Science and AI at University of Rome La Sapienza. Throughout the course, we cover DNS, VPNs, SSH and Firewalls with challenging problems and homeworks to keep students stimulated. For a complete list of topics, visit this link.

I teach this course since A.Y. 2017/2018.

ICT Infrastructure Security

This course is divided in 3 sub-areas, one of which is the one I teach: Architecture Security. In this area, the student will learn the basics theory behind side-channel attacks. It will develop an in-depth knowledge of computer arhcitectures, developing a critical sense of what really happens in a modern x86 pipeline, uncovering Spectre and Meltown vulnerabilities. As an appendix, we explore some basic Access Control.

The target audience are students at the 1-st year of their B.Sc. in ICT Engineering at University of Rome Tor Vergata.
